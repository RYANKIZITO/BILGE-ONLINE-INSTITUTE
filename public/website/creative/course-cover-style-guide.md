# Bilge Website Course Cover Style Guide

This guide is for the public-facing Bilge website only.

## Purpose

Use these image directions for programme cards, featured programme sections, programme detail banners, and promotional website surfaces.

## Visual Intent

- Premium
- Academic
- Trustworthy
- Elegant
- Institutional
- Modern
- Conversion-focused

## Core Brand Direction

- Deep charcoal and black carry the authority
- Ivory, cream, and soft white keep the covers readable
- Matte gold is the accent, never the main reading color
- Lighting should feel refined and editorial, not flashy
- Composition should feel calm, expensive, and structured

## Primary Cover Format

- Aspect ratio: `4:5`
- Use for catalogue cards, featured programme cards, and promotional programme blocks

## Secondary Cover Format

- Aspect ratio: `16:9`
- Use for programme detail hero banners and website campaign sections

## Safe Layout Guidance

- Keep the main visual subject centered or slightly offset
- Leave a clean text-safe area in the upper-left or lower-left third
- Avoid placing key visual elements against the edges
- Keep backgrounds uncluttered so website overlays stay readable

## Shared Master Prompt

`Premium academic course cover for Bilge Online Institute, elegant institutional branding, modern online education, deep charcoal and ivory palette, matte gold accents, refined editorial lighting, world-class institute aesthetic, clean professional composition, trustworthy and polished, room for title text, no watermark, no clutter`

## Negative Prompt

`cartoon, childish, cheap template, low quality, blurry, noisy collage, busy background, random icons, fake 3d shapes, neon overload, metallic gold shine, distorted faces, watermark, extra text, logo duplication`
